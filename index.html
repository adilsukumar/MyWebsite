What I Do

Honestly? I just get really curious about stuff. Sometimes that means building a robot for fun, diving into random topics, spotting patterns in chaos, or automating tiny parts of my life just because I can. I’ve taught myself to code, built things that broke, built things that worked, and gone down way too many internet rabbit holes. I like connecting dots between things that don’t seem connected — psychology, tech, biology, design, whatever. I don’t have a single “thing.” I just like making stuff, learning out loud, and turning ideas into something real — even if it’s messy or weird..

Who I Am

I’m someone who cares — sometimes too much, sometimes quietly. I overthink, get excited about odd ideas, and dive into projects that don’t always make sense at first. I’m curious about how people live, how systems work, how stories unfold, and how small details shift everything. I’ve always been a “connect-the-dots-my-own-way” kind of person, even if it means messing up. I’m still figuring things out, but I know I want to keep learning, building, helping, and creating things that matter — even if they’re messy or unfinished. That’s me, pretty much.

Things I’ve Built

I’ve made a lot of things — some with code, some with cardboard, some with questions I couldn’t stop thinking about. Projects that tried to make life simpler, or weirder, or just more interesting. Stuff like hacked-together automations, half-working robots, trading bots that actually worked, and a few ideas that started with “this is probably a bad idea.” I’ve spent months learning how systems work — from the stock market to smart homes — and just as much time volunteering, writing, and trying to make sense of people and how we learn. Recently, I’ve been building a finance app — probably the first of its kind, built around behavior, design, and a totally different way of thinking about money. A lot of what I build doesn’t have a clear label. But it’s all driven by the same thing: curiosity, and the need to turn thoughts into things.
